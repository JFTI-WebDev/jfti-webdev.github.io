Company Overview

J.F. Taylor, Inc. is a leading provider of advanced mission and training systems for the U.S. Department of Defense. With over 40 years of experience in design, engineering, and manufacturing, J.F. Taylor delivers innovative, high-quality solutions that enhance military readiness and ensure the safety of warfighters. Headquartered in Great Mills, Maryland, with a flagship office in Beavercreek, Ohio, the company is committed to supporting the evolving needs of U.S. military personnel and allied forces worldwide.

In 2025, J.F. Taylor expanded its capabilities through the acquisition of Bugeye Technologies, a trusted supplier of complex mechanical and electrical assemblies, simulation products, and turnkey engineering services. Based near St. Louis, Missouri, Bugeye Technologies, now operating as Bugeye Technologies Powered by JF Taylor, brings over two decades of expertise in Commercial-Off-the-Shelf (COTS) flight simulation and custom-fabricated solutions, further strengthening J.F. Taylor’s position as a comprehensive defense solutions provider.

Together, J.F. Taylor and Bugeye Technologies are united in their mission to deliver cutting-edge, reliable, and efficient systems that meet the critical demands of modern defense operations.

    OUR HISTORY

    J.F. Taylor, Inc. Through the Years

    • 1983

      Flight Test Support

      J.F. Taylor's history began supporting the Naval Air Test Center by providing engineering and test support of Identification systems in Naval and Marine Corp aircraft.

    • 1984

      1st Prime Contract - IFF Flight Testing

    • 1986

      Data Reduction/Analysis

      J.F. Taylor developed their first flight-worthy data collection instrumentation, as well as a data reduction and analysis capability to support flight and laboratory testing.

    • 1986

      Laboratory Test Simulator

      J.F. Taylor designed, fabricated, and delivered a high-fidelity simulator/stimulator for the Patrol Avionics Test Laboratory, simulating the functionality of the P-3C sensor stations and the environment with which it operates.

    • 1991

      Full-Fidelity Simulation and Testing

      J.F. Taylor began its support of the Manned Flight Simulator, Simulation Division with the development and operation of high-fidelity simulations of multiple Navy and Marine Corp aircraft, providing avionics modeling, hardware-in-the-loop engineering, electrical and mechanical design, and fabrication.

    • 1997

      100 Employees

    • 1999

      High Fidelity Flight Trainer Products

      J.F. Taylor Trainer Products Group begins with the design, fabrication, and delivery of two AH-1W High Fidelity Flight Trainer to Taiwan. Our investments included the establishment of high-bay manufacturing space with mechanical and electrical fabrication tooling.

    • 2004

      ISO 9001 Certified

    • 2008

      Aircraft Cable Harnesses and Modification Kits

      J.F. Taylor turned their design and fabrication skills to support the fabrication of aircraft flight-worthy cable harnesses and electro-mechanical aircraft modification kits, with the delivery of CNS/ATM kits for the E-2C, C-2, and CH/MH-53.

    • 2012

      Unmanned & Maritime Systems

      J.F. Taylor began its support of unmanned and maritime systems with the engineering design support and fabrication of FireScout and UCAS control stations.

    • 2013

      AS9100 Certified

    • 2016

      1st Avionics System Contract - Mission Computer Alternative (MCA)

    • 2019

      500+ Employee Company

      Over the past 35 years, we have grown from a one-person operation to a company with over 500 employees and $150 million in annual revenues.

    • 2022

      New 100,000 Square Foot Facility

      On September 19, 2022, JF Taylor opened its Cecil's Mill Facility in Great Mills, MD which includes 70,000 square feet of high-bay, fabrication, and laboratory space. This one-of-a-kind facility in the NAS Patuxent River area supports over 100 electrical and mechanical fabricators and support personnel and over 50 engineers, software developers, and management personnel.

    • 2025

      JF Taylor, Inc. Acquired Bugeye Technologies, Inc.

      On April 7, 2025, JF Taylor, Inc. announced the acquisition of Bugeye Technologies, Inc. This strategic move expands JF Taylor’s capabilities, enabling the company to deliver a more comprehensive suite of training and operational solutions to meet the evolving needs of today’s warfighters.

    Simulation & Training Systems

    Pilots trust us to help them learn

    Our products include manned and unmanned flight simulators that train today's pilots on the most advanced technologies in the world. We also design and manufacture trainers for Army ground vehicles, Navy shipboard systems, and military maintenance and weapons training systems.

    Avionics Systems & Aircraft Modifications

    J.F. Taylor designs, fabricates, and integrates military technologies that include communication, navigation, computational, and other warfighter critical systems for integration into fixed and rotary aircraft. In support of flight testing, we design, build, and install custom test and instrumentation equipment to record critical performance data. We also fabricate and install aircraft flight-worthy cables and kits, as well as performing other aircraft modifications.

    Unmanned & Maritime Systems

    J.F. Taylor designs, prototypes, tests, and fields complex Command, Control, Computers, Communications, Cyber, Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance systems within the US Navy.

    Ground-Based Systems

    J.F. Taylor designs, fabricates, and integrates military technologies that include communication, navigation, and other warfighter critical systems for installation into ground-based control systems, test facilities, and laboratories

    NEWS: J.F. Taylor Acquires CCAT

    JF Taylor, Inc Expands Solutions Offering with Acquisition of Specialized USAF Contractor Clear Creek Applied Technologies

    Great Mills, MD – [April 1, 2024] – JF Taylor announced today the acquisition of Clear Creek Applied Technologies (CCAT), a software solutions company with offices in Dayton, OH and Ft. Walton Beach, FL. CCAT specializes in mission-critical simulation and training solutions development as well as test and evaluation. This strategic alignment strengthens JF Taylor’s position as a leading provider of cutting-edge training systems and defense solutions to the Department of Defense (DoD) and its international partners. "We are excited to welcome CCAT and its talented team to the JF Taylor family," said Wayne Taylor, President. "CCAT 's proven expertise in developing and delivering high-quality and proven mission and training solutions aligns perfectly with our mission to provide our customers with the most advanced and comprehensive support capabilities. This acquisition broadens our portfolio and positions us to better serve the evolving needs of our customers in the digital age."

    CCAT brings over 18 years of experience in the defense industry, specializing in the development and deployment of mission-critical software applications and T&E services for various US Navy, Air Force, and foreign partner programs. The company's team of highly skilled engineers, developers, and analysts boasts a deep understanding of its customers’ unique requirements and security protocols, ensuring the delivery of secure, reliable, and interoperable software solutions alongside mission critical test and evaluation services.

    "Joining forces with JF Taylor presents an incredible opportunity to leverage our combined expertise and resources to make an even greater impact on the DoD's mission success," said Walt March, President/CEO at CCAT. "We are confident that this partnership will enable us to deliver even more innovative and effective solutions to our customers while expanding our reach and capabilities."

    The acquisition of CCAT underscores JF Taylor’s commitment to providing its customers with a comprehensive suite of training and mission support solutions. By integrating CCAT's development capabilities with its existing expertise in highly complex military systems, JF Taylor will be able to offer a wider range of solutions that address the full spectrum of the warfighter’s training and operational needs.

    NEWS: JF Taylor Acquires Bugeye Technologies

    JF Taylor Expands Solutions Offering with Acquisition of Specialized DoD Solutions Provider, Bugeye Technologies

    Great Mills, MD – [April 7, 2025] – JF Taylor Inc. announced today the acquisition of Bugeye Technologies Inc. (Bugeye), an engineering and manufacturing solutions company located in Union, Missouri. Bugeye specializes in high quality simulated hardware and custom electro-mechanical assemblies for aviation and simulation/training applications. This strategic alignment strengthens JF Taylor’s position as a leading small-business provider of cutting-edge training systems and defense solutions for the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) and its international partners.

    "We are excited to welcome Bugeye and its talented team to the JF Taylor family," said Chris Wasniak, JF Taylor chief operating officer. "Bugeye's proven performance in developing and delivering on-time, high-quality, and best-value solutions aligns perfectly with JF Taylor’s mission to provide our customers with the most advanced, comprehensive, and cost-effective readiness capabilities. This acquisition broadens our portfolio, increases our capacity, and positions us to better serve the needs of our customers in the rapidly evolving modern age."

    Bugeye brings more than 22 years of experience in the defense industry as a supplier of realistic, serviceable and affordable products in the flight simulation industry. They design, engineer, and manufacture a variety of cockpits, control sticks, throttles, cyclics, collectives, yokes, rudder pedals and control loading at multiple levels of fidelity, from desktop to full mission trainers. They support more than 20 platforms that range from rotary wing aircraft including AH-64, H-60, and H-47, to fixed wing aircraft with F-16, F-22, F-35, and many more air and ground platforms.

    "Joining forces with JF Taylor presents an incredible opportunity to leverage our combined expertise and resources to make an even greater impact on our customers’ mission success," said Kyle Zick, Bugeye president. "We are enthusiastic that this partnership will enable us to deliver even more innovative and effective solutions while expanding our capacity and capabilities."

    The acquisition of Bugeye underscores JF Taylor’s commitment to investing in continuous capabilities enhancement to ultimately provide customers with a comprehensive suite of training and mission support solutions. By integrating Bugeye’s design and manufacturing capabilities with its existing expertise in highly complex military systems, JF Taylor will be able to offer a wider range of solutions that addresses the full spectrum of the warfighter’s training and operational needs.
