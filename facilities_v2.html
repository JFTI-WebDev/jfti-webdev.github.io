Facilities Overview

J.F. Taylor is headquartered in Lexington Park, Maryland, within three miles of NAVAIR’s Patuxent River Naval Air Station. Our facilities have over 150,000 square feet of office, laboratory, manufacturing, and warehouse space. Within our manufacturing facilities, over 50,000 square feet is high bay space, allowing us to engage in large-scale manufacturing and fabrication work.

We have ten facilities, including our Main Office, Trainer Products Facility (TPF), Engineering and Manufacturing Facility (EMF), Engineering and Conference Facility (ECF), Mission Systems Facility (MSF), and three additional facilities that house simulation development teams. Each of our fabrication facilities is populated with fully integrated project teams.

These teams include project managers, engineers, and technicians that are equipped with state-of-the-art engineering design tools and manufacturing equipment. We believe that integrating the engineering and manufacturing disciplines is critical for increased efficiencies and quality.

Engineering and Manufacturing Facility (EMF)

Our Engineering and Manufacturing Facility (EMF) supports the manufacture and fabrication of prototypes and the high-volume production of aircraft and maritime kits and components. The EMF is a 28,000-square-foot facility, with 20,000 square feet of high bay space. The EMF’s mechanical and electrical shops are outfitted with the latest in multi-axis CNC routers, press brakes, milling machines, hydraulic shears, wire markers, wire cutters, pneumatic crimpers, label printers, pneumatic rivet machines, water jet, 3D printer, and commercial sewing machines, as well as a full array of saws, drills, sanders/grinders, air tools, measuring devices, and paint/powder coat booths. Our Quality System is certified to AS9100:2016.

Trainer Products Facility (TPF)

Our Trainer Products Facility (TPF) is a hybrid office and manufacturing space, which houses 32,000 square feet of mechanical and electrical shops dedicated to producing trainer devices. The TPF has over 20,000 square feet of high bay space to develop multiple, simultaneous simulator programs. The mechanical shop is capable of replicating aircraft assemblies, components, and all hardware required for high fidelity simulators and contains state-of-the-art equipment for machining, sheet metal, steel, welding, painting, iriditing, mechanical assembly, and 3D-printed components. The electrical shop fabricates and installs cable assemblies and wire harnesses and houses resources for soldering, wire marking, wire cutting, pneumatic crimping, label printing, and cable testing, with dedicated ESD-safe work areas.

Test Instrumentation Lab

Our instrumentation laboratory has fabrication capabilities for test equipment, allowing us to design and develop advanced on-board instrumentation for aircraft. The lab’s resources and capabilities include self-printed circuit board design and assembly, a metal shop, FPGAL/PAL design, and compiler/assembler/linker/debugger tools. The lab is located at the Engineering and Conference Facility (ECF).
